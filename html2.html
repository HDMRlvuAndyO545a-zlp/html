  • Servings: 8 Servings
  • Servings: 8 Servings
  • Cost: $27.45-$30.36

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces ($0.66) rice vermicelli ($0.66)
  • 8 rice wrappers 8.5 inch diameter ($4)
  • 8 large, cooked shrimp peeled, deveined and cut in half ($11.68-14.59)
  • 1 1/3 tablespoons of chopped fresh Thai basil ($0.88)
  • 3 tablespoons of chopped fresh mint leaves ($3.96)
  • 3 tablespoons of chopped fresh cilantro ($0.60)
  • 2 chopped leaves lettuce ($2.96)
  • 4 teaspoons of fish sauce ($0.33)
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh lime juice ($0.99)
  • 1 minced clove of garlic ($0.76)
  • 2 tablespoons of white sugar ($0.16)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic chili sauce ($0.02)
  • 3 tablespoons of hoisin sauce ($0.42)
  • 1 teaspoon of finely chopped peanuts ($0.03)

Directions

  1. Bring a medium saucepan of water to boil. Boil rice vermicelli 3 to 5 minutes, or until al dente, and drain.
  2. Fill a large bowl with warm water. Dip one wrapper into the hot water for 1 second to soften. Lay wrapper flat. In a row across the center, place 2 shrimp halves, a handful of vermicelli, basil, mint, cilantro and lettuce, leaving about 2 inches uncovered on each side. Fold uncovered sides inward, then tightly roll the wrapper, beginning at the end with the lettuce. Repeat with remaining ingredients.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the fish sauce, water, lime juice, garlic, sugar and chili sauce.
  4. In another small bowl, mix the hoisin sauce and peanuts.
  5. Serve rolled spring rolls with the fish sauce and hoisin sauce mixtures.

Note:

The fish sauce, rice vermicelli, chili garlic sauce, hoisin sauce, and rice wrappers can be found at Asian food markets.

The shrimp accounts for most of the cost and the recipe can be altered according to your budget

If so desired make one sauce but in a larger quantity
